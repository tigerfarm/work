Katakana

Katakana is phonic based, most often used to write words from foreign words using a phonic transcription. Example, my name Stacy ステイシー, pronounced su ti shī(i). The Thai language is also phonic based, and english names are phonically transcribed in Thai, example, David is written in Thai as เดวิด.

Kanji

The Japanese characters Kanji, are not phonic based. Kanji are derived from Chinese characters, for example, the word for country is the same in Kanji as simplified Chinese, 国. Simplification of Kanji, followed simplification of the Chinese characters in China. In Japanese, most Kanji characters are associated with a single Japanese syllable.
